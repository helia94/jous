# Jous SEO Strategy: Conversation Cards

## Goal

Get `jous.app` onto the first Google results page for **conversation cards** by building topical authority around a sharper position than the current market:

> Jous is the largest open-source, randomly served conversation card app. It is not self-help, not therapy cosplay, and not cringe.

The typo **conversatin cards** should not be the primary target. Treat it as a Search Console signal only if it appears later. The real target is **conversation cards** and its long-tail variants.

## Positioning

Most competitors frame conversation cards as self-improvement, intimacy work, couple therapy, party games, or polished gift products. Jous should own a different lane:

- **Largest**: a very large collection of questions and prompts, with transparent counts.
- **Open source**: the question set and product philosophy are inspectable, forkable, and remixable.
- **Most random**: randomness is the feature, not a bug. The point is to get unexpected prompts, not optimized emotional homework.
- **No self-help**: no promise to fix relationships, heal people, or create fake vulnerability on demand.
- **Not cringe**: plain language, weird/funny/deep prompts mixed together, no inspirational tone.

Short version:

> Open-source conversation cards for random, non-cringe questions.

Alternative title language:

- `Open-Source Conversation Cards`
- `Random Conversation Cards, Without the Self-Help`
- `The Largest Open-Source Conversation Card App`
- `Conversation Cards That Are Random, Weird, Deep, and Not Cringe`

## Primary Page

Create a dedicated page:

`/conversation-cards`

Recommended metadata:

- **Title**: `Conversation Cards | Open-Source, Random, Not Cringe | Jous`
- **Meta description**: `Jous is an open-source conversation card app with a large random question set. No self-help scripts, no cringe prompts, just cards for real conversations.`
- **H1**: `Open-Source Conversation Cards`
- **Primary CTA**: `Draw a random card`

The page should include:

- A live/random card preview above the fold.
- A clear count of available cards/questions.
- A direct statement that the prompts are open source.
- A few examples of random cards, including funny, weird, casual, and deeper prompts.
- A comparison against physical decks, printable PDFs, and self-help card apps.
- Links to the source repository or public prompt dataset if available.
- FAQ content that answers search intent directly.

## Core Search Intent

People searching **conversation cards** may want different things:

- A card deck to buy.
- A printable PDF.
- An online/app version.
- Conversation starters for couples, friends, families, groups, or dates.
- A free alternative to paid decks.
- A less awkward way to start talking.

Jous should satisfy the online/free/random/open-source intent better than anyone else.

## Underserved Market Expansion Bets

Do not choose countries only by population. For Jous, the better question is:

> Where do people already use mobile/social tools heavily, like casual games or prompts, but do not have a strong local, open-source, non-cringe conversation-card product?

Based on that lens, the best expansion bets are not the same as the biggest internet markets.

### Best Near-Term Bet: Philippines

Priority: **High**

Recommended language approach:

- Start with English.
- Add Filipino/Tagalog after validating search demand and usage.
- Later consider Cebuano only if analytics show meaningful usage outside Tagalog/English-heavy regions.

Why the Philippines is attractive:

- Very high social media and messenger usage.
- Strong mobile-first behavior.
- English is widely usable, so Jous can test the market before a full localization project.
- Social, friend-group, dating, family, and party use cases all fit naturally.
- Existing products appear mostly like imported physical decks or marketplace listings, not a strong local open-source online category.

Likely positioning:

## Content Principles

Jous content should sound like Jous:

- Short, plain, and specific.
- No wellness language.
- No fake emotional profundity.
- No generic SEO filler.
- No claims like "transform your relationship" or "unlock deeper connection."
- Use concrete examples of actual cards.
- Admit randomness and weirdness as product strengths.

Avoid:

- "Spark meaningful conversations" as the main phrase.
- "Deepen your connection" as a repeated claim.
- Therapy-coded language.
- Over-explaining how to talk to humans.

Use:

- "Draw a card."
- "Get a random question."
- "No deck to carry."
- "No self-help script."
- "Use it with friends, dates, family, coworkers, or strangers."
- "Some cards are dumb. Some are deep. That is the point."
